Kwabena Adu Agyemang, MS., BSc.

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at New Mexico State University, pursuing an interdisciplinary program with Curriculum & Instruction. My interests include CS Education, AI Pedagogy, Human–Computer Interaction, Learning Technologies, and STEM Education. My current work examines how computer science teachers develop and integrate AI in their teaching practice, documenting the skills, values, and classroom moves that make AI instruction effective. Previously, I authored an MS thesis on student practices for using AI learning assistants and co-authored a 2025 article in PLOS ONE. Methodologically, I use qualitative and mixed-methods approaches to translate classroom realities into actionable guidance. My goal is to support teachers and learners with practical, ethical, and research-grounded ways to use AI.
